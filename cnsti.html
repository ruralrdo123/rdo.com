Constitution of RURAL DEVELOPMENT ORGANISATION (RDO)

Article I: Name and Location

1.1. The name of the organization shall be RURAL DEVELOPMENT ORGANISATION, hereinafter referred to as "the Organization".
1.2. The Organization shall have its principal office located at- Malibari Bazar, Vill- No 1 Malibari Pather, P.O- Malibari Bazar, P.S-Boko, Dist- Kamrup,Pin- 781136, and may establish additional offices or branches as deemed necessary by the Board of Directors.

Article II: Mission and Objectives

2.1. Mission: The mission of the Organization shall be At the core of RDO's mission is the belief that every rural community deserves an opportunity to thrive. We aim to empower these communities by fostering sustainable development, preserving cultural heritage, and enhancing the overall quality of life for residents.
2.2. Objectives: The objectives of the Organization shall include but not be limited to:

  • Rural Education Empowerment
  • Rural Environmentel Development
  • Rural communication Enhancement
  • Rural Financial Empowerment
  • Rural Cultural Revitalization
  • Sustainable Agriculture For Rural Livelihoods

    Article IV: Governance

    4.1. Board of Directors: The Organization shall be governed by a Board of Directors, consisting of 13 members elected by the members {except president,secretary and manager} of the Organization.
    4.2. Duties and Powers: The Board of Directors shall have the authority to,

    Powers

  • Policy Development: Directors develop and approve policies that guide the organization's operations, ensuring compliance with legal and ethical standards.
  • Financial Oversight: Directors oversee the organization's financial affairs, including budgeting, financial reporting, and internal controls, to ensure fiscal responsibility and accountability.
  • Risk Management: Directors identify and assess risks to the organization and develop strategies to mitigate them, safeguarding the organization's assets and reputation.
  • Board Development: Directors recruit, evaluate, and support board members, ensuring a diverse and skilled board capable of fulfilling its responsibilities.
  • Legal Compliance: Directors ensure that the organization complies with all relevant laws, regulations, and ethical standards, and address any legal or regulatory issues that arise. and shall act in the best interests of the Organization
    • .

    Duties

  • Decision-Making: Directors have the authority to make decisions on behalf of the organization, including approving budgets, strategic plans, policies, and major initiatives
  • Appointment and Removal: Directors have the power to appoint and remove officers, executives, and other key personnel, ensuring effective leadership and governance
  • Resource Allocation: Directors allocate resources, including financial resources, personnel, and other assets, to support the organization's mission and objectives
  • Representation: Directors represent the organization to external stakeholders, including government agencies, funders, partners, and the public, advocating for the organization's interests and promoting its mission.
  • Legal Authority: Directors have the legal authority to enter into contracts, agreements, and other legal obligations on behalf of the organization, subject to appropriate oversight and approval processes
  • Strategic Direction: Directors set the strategic direction of the organization, guiding its long-term growth, development, and sustainability
  • Risk Management: Directors assess and manage risks to the organization, taking actions to mitigate risks and seize opportunities that align with the organization's mission and objectives
  • Monitoring and Oversight: Directors monitor the organization's performance and operations, providing oversight and guidance to management to ensure effective and ethical leadership
  • Compliance: Directors ensure that the organization complies with all applicable laws, regulations, and ethical standards, taking corrective action as needed to address non-compliance
    • 4.3. Meetings: The Board of Directors shall hold regular meetings will be held every two month, and special meetings may be called as necessary.

    5.2. Duties: The duties of each officer shall be

    President:

    • Provide leadership and direction to the organization.
    • Chair board meetings and represent the organization externally.
    • Develop and implement strategic plans.
    • Oversee fundraising efforts and ensure financial stability.
    • Monitor organizational performance and address issues as needed.

    Secretary:

    • Maintain accurate records of board meetings.
    • Facilitate communication between board members and stakeholders.
    • Ensure compliance with legal and regulatory requirements.
    • Coordinate logistics for board meetings.
    • Support board orientation and training.

    Manager/Director:

    • Develop and implement operational plans.
    • Manage the organization's budget and resources.
    • Oversee program implementation and evaluation.
    • Supervise staff and support their professional development.
    • Cultivate partnerships and funding opportunities.

    Treasurer:

    • Oversee financial management and reporting.
    • Develop and monitor the organization's budget.
    • Ensure compliance with financial regulations.
    • Manage investments and financial risks.
    • Provide financial information and support to the board.

    Advisors:

    • Provide strategic guidance and expertise.
    • Offer mentorship and support to board members and staff.
    • Facilitate networking and connections.
    • Assist with problem-solving and decision-making.
    • Conduct research and evaluation to inform organizational strategies.
    .
    5.3. Terms of Office: The term of office for officers shall be 2 years, and officers may be re-elected for consecutive terms.

    Article VI: Finances

    6.1. Financial Management: The Board of Directors shall be responsible for the financial management of the Organization, including budgeting, fundraising, and financial reporting.
    6.2. Financial Year: The fiscal year of the Organization shall be 2nd Jan to 30 Dec.
    6.3. Audits: An independent audit of the Organization's finances shall be conducted annually.

    Article VII: Amendments

    7.1. Amendment Process: This constitution may be amended by a 71% vote of the members present at a meeting called for that purpose, provided that notice of the proposed amendment has been given to the members at least 10 days in advance.

    Article IX: Miscellaneous

    9.1. Conflict of Interest: Directors and officers of the Organization shall disclose any potential conflicts of interest and refrain from participating in decisions where they have a personal interest.
    9.2. Indemnification: The Organization shall indemnify and hold harmless its directors, officers, employees, and agents to the fullest extent permitted by law.

    Article X: Ratification

    10.1. Ratification: This constitution shall be ratified by a [Percentage]% vote of the members present at a meeting called for that purpose.
